The First Women's
College Hackathon

Like our school's founder, we reckon ourselves pioneers: change-makers unafraid to be the first to do great work. Mount Holyoke is the oldest women's college in the United States, and, true to this spirit, we were the first women's college to host a hackathon. HackHolyoke is a 24-hour hackathon that welcomes hackers from around the country to be fearless, to truly be innovative, and to develop brilliantly new and disruptive technologies.





50-50 Gender Ratio

In the spirit of breaking grounds, HackHolyoke was the first to achieve and continues to achieve a one to one ratio of female to male hackers, emphasizing the important notion that all genders can succeed in engineering.

FAQ

When?

The evening of Friday, April 3, concluding 24 hours later on Saturday, April 4. See above for our tentative schedule!

Where?

Mount Holyoke College in the always beautiful Pioneer Valley of western Massachusetts.

Who can register?

Everyone with a hacking spirit is welcome!

What if I don't know how to code?

You've chosen the best place to learn! We will ensure that your first hacking experience is welcoming, helpful, and motivating. Our awesome mentors would love to hear your questions and help you through your first hack.

What about team size?

You can work in a team of up to four. Don't have a team yet? Don't worry! There will be time to form teams after registration, and we will help you match with other hackers.

What should I bring?

All you need is a student ID, a laptop + charger, sleeping bag and your hacking spirit! We'll take care of the rest.

What about travel?

At this time, we cannot reimburse any travel. Our deepest appologies!

What if I want to hack hardware?

Awesome! Here is what we'll be providing!

More questions?

Please feel free to contact us at hackholyoke@gmail.com or reach out to us on facebook or twitter. We'd love to hear from you!
